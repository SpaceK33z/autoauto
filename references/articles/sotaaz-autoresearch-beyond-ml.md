## Overview

This article extends the autoresearch pattern—a methodology based on iterative cycles of modification, execution, evaluation, and retention—beyond machine learning applications to practical developer and marketer challenges.

## Main Content

The piece covers three non-ML optimization problems:

1. **System Prompt Optimization** — Using automated techniques to enhance LLM prompt accuracy
2. **Code Performance Optimization** — Strategies for faster builds and smaller bundle sizes
3. **Landing Page Copy Optimization** — Algorithmic approaches to identify high-converting headlines

Each case includes executable code: `prepare.py` files, starter `train.py` implementations, and `program.md` templates ready for immediate use.

## Series Context

This represents Part 4 of a four-part series examining autoresearch methodology:
- Part 1: Architecture fundamentals
- Part 2: Hands-on experimentation
- Part 3: ML domain applications
- Part 4: Non-ML extensions (current)

## Key Insight

The article emphasizes that the ratchet loop optimization framework proves domain-agnostic—"the ratchet loop — modify, run, evaluate, keep or discard — doesn't care what you're optimizing."
